      Behcet
        Middle East
        Turkey
        diagnostic criteria
          3/3 major is definitive
          2/3 major + 2 minor is pretty convincing
          MAJOR:
            oral and genital ulcers
              nearly all Behcet patients have oral ulcers
            uveitis OR retinal vasculitis
              eye involvement in 3/4 of Behcet
            skin lesions
              erythema nodosum
              positive pathergy test
                hypersensitivity pustule 1-2 days after sterile needle prick
          MINOR:
            GI lesions
              small bowel ulcers
              IBD-like symptoms
            thrombophlebitis
              large venous thrombosis
            CNS lesions
              dural VST with elevated ICP
              inflammatory lesions in the brainstem
              if any neurologic signs
                MRI
                LP
                  elevated neutrophils
                  elevated IgG protein
            FH
          ALSO:
            renal involvement
              glomerulonephritis
        when you see (retinitis + BRVO ± uveitis)
          think Behcet
        HLA-B51 present in more than half
        treatment
          topical steroids
          cycloplegia
          prednisolone 1 mg/kg/d to start
          transition to SSIMT
            cyclosporine
            interferon alpha
            TNF inhibitors
      GPA
        treatment
          per Jabs
            rituximab is highly effective in ANCA+ conditions
              see NEJM 2010:363:221
